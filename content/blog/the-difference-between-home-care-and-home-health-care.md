When seeking support for an aging loved one or recovering patient, families often ask: *“What’s the difference between home care and home health care?”* Although the terms sound similar, they serve very different purposes — and understanding the distinction can help you make the right choice for your needs.

### **What Is Home Care?**

**Home care** refers to **non-medical assistance** provided in the comfort of one’s home. It’s ideal for seniors who want to maintain independence but need help with daily tasks.
**Services may include:**

* Personal care (bathing, grooming, dressing)
* Meal preparation and light housekeeping
* Medication reminders
* Companionship
* Transportation to appointments

At **Vitalis HealthCare**, our compassionate caregivers support clients not just physically, but emotionally — helping them age gracefully in familiar surroundings.

### **What Is Home Health Care?**

**Home health care**, on the other hand, involves **medical care delivered at home by licensed professionals** under a doctor’s order. It is typically prescribed after surgery, illness, or hospital discharge.
**Services may include:**

* Skilled nursing (wound care, injections, monitoring vitals)
* Physical, occupational, or speech therapy
* Chronic disease management
* Post-hospital recovery support

Home health care is often covered by Medicare, Medicaid, or private insurance, depending on eligibility.

### **Which One Do You Need?**

* Choose **home care** for help with daily living and companionship.
* Choose **home health care** for medical treatment or recovery after a health event.

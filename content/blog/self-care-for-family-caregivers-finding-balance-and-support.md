Being a family caregiver is an act of love—but it can also be emotionally and physically draining. When you're caring for someone else, it's easy to put your own needs last. At **Vitalis HealthCare**, we believe caregivers deserve care too. Here's how you can prioritize your well-being while supporting your loved one.

### **Why Self-Care Matters**

Burnout is common among family caregivers. You may feel guilty taking time for yourself, but the truth is: *you can’t pour from an empty cup.* When you’re rested and supported, you’re better equipped to care for others with patience, energy, and compassion.

### **Simple Self-Care Practices That Make a Difference**

1. **Take Breaks Without Guilt** Even 15 minutes to walk, journal, or sip tea in silence can reset your energy.
2. **Nourish Your Body** Don’t skip meals or live on coffee. Hydrate, eat nutritious food, and try to get enough sleep.
3. **Ask for Help** You don’t have to do it all alone. Accept help from friends, family—or professional caregivers like those at Vitalis.
4. **Connect With Others** Join a caregiver support group, in person or online. Sharing your experience helps reduce isolation and emotional fatigue.
5. **Schedule “Me Time”** Mark it on your calendar like an appointment. Whether it’s reading, gardening, or attending a faith service—protect that time.

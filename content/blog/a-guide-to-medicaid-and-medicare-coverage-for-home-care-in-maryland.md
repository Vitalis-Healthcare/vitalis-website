Navigating healthcare coverage can feel overwhelming—especially when it comes to understanding what **Medicaid** and **Medicare** cover for home care services. At **Vitalis HealthCare**, we’re committed to helping families in Maryland make informed decisions about their care options. Here’s a simple guide to help you understand how these two programs can support your loved ones at home.

### **What Does Medicare Cover for Home Care?**

**Medicare** is a federal health insurance program for:

* People aged 65 and older
* Certain younger individuals with disabilities
* People with End-Stage Renal Disease

**Medicare may cover home health care** under certain conditions, such as:

* The care must be **medically necessary** and ordered by a doctor.
* The patient must be **homebound** (unable to leave home without assistance).
* Care must be provided by a **Medicare-certified home health agency**.

**Covered services may include:**

* Skilled nursing care
* Physical, occupational, or speech therapy
* Medical social services
* Certain home health aide services (on a part-time basis)

**What’s not covered?**Medicare generally does **not cover 24/7 care**, **meal delivery**, **personal care** (like bathing and dressing), or **homemaker services** unless they are part of the skilled care plan.

### **What Does Medicaid Cover for Home Care in Maryland?**

**Medicaid** is a state and federally funded program that provides health coverage for low-income individuals.
In Maryland, Medicaid, under the Medicaid Waiver programs, offers **more extensive coverage for home care** through programs like:

#### **✅ Community First Choice (CFC):**

* Helps eligible individuals receive **personal assistance services** in their home.
* Covers activities of daily living (ADLs), like bathing, eating, and mobility.

#### **✅ Home and Community-Based Options Waiver (CO Waiver):**

* Designed to help older adults and individuals with disabilities **avoid nursing home placement**.
* Provides both **medical** and **non-medical** in-home services.

#### **✅ Medical Day Care & Personal Care Programs:**

* Offer structured care, health monitoring, and social interaction for eligible seniors during the day.

### **How to Apply**

* **Medicare**: Enrollment is usually automatic at age 65, but you can apply through [www.medicare.gov](https://www.medicare.gov).
* **Medicaid in Maryland**: Apply via [Maryland Health Connection](https://www.marylandhealthconnection.gov/) or visit your local Department of Social Services.

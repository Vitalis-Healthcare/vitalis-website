Aging at home offers comfort and independence—but it also requires careful attention to safety. At **Vitalis HealthCare**, we’re committed to helping Maryland seniors live confidently and securely in the homes they love. Here are some essential tips to help older adults stay safe at home:

### **1. Prevent Falls**

Falls are a leading cause of injury for seniors. To reduce the risk:

* Keep floors clutter-free and secure rugs with non-slip backing.
* Install grab bars in bathrooms and handrails on stairways.
* Ensure good lighting in hallways, stairs, and entrances.

### **2. Medication Management**

Proper medication use is vital:

* Store medications in clearly labeled containers.
* Set daily reminders or use a pill organizer.
* Review prescriptions regularly with a healthcare provider.

### **3. Fire and Carbon Monoxide Safety**

* Install smoke and CO detectors and test them monthly.
* Avoid using space heaters without automatic shutoff features.
* Never leave stoves or ovens unattended while cooking.

### **4. Stay Connected**

* Keep emergency contacts easily accessible.
* Schedule regular check-ins with family, neighbors, or caregivers.
* Consider using a medical alert system for added peace of mind.

### **5. Home Security**

* Keep doors and windows locked.
* Be cautious of strangers and scams—never give personal information over the phone.
* Install peepholes or security cameras for extra protection.

### **6. Professional Support**

* Hire licensed caregivers for help with daily activities, medication reminders, or companionship.

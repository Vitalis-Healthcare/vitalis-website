When it comes to hiring a caregiver, choosing one who is a right fit for you or a loved one is very important as it can greatly contribute to their comfort, general well being, safety and overall satisfaction.
Before hiring a caregiver, here are a few helpful tips to consider and make use of.

1. **Verify Their Qualifications and Experiences:** When in the process of hiring a caregiver, it is important to look through their qualifications and previous experiences. This will confirm that they have the necessary training and expertise required.
2. **Outline and Consider Your Specific Needs Against Their Experience:** When choosing a caregiver, it is important to outline the specific needs you want them to meet and the roles you would like them to fulfill. This way, you are able to clearly communicate this with them and receive feedback on whether or not they are capable of filling the role. Doing this also enables you to weigh their capacity to handle your expectations against their qualifications and previous experiences.
3. **Assess For Compatibility:** As you interview caregivers, beyond checking for their qualification and experience, it is also vital to assess their compatibility with you or your loved one. This involves observing things like their language fluency level and obvious behavioral or personality traits and assessing if they are compatible with the person they would be providing care for. This is a significant determinant to the convenience and receptiveness of the patient to the caregiver and their caregiving.
4. **Follow up on References:** After looking through their qualifications and experiences, it is important to also contact and follow up on their listed references to confirm their experiences, work ethic, capacity and reliability. Hiring a caregiver through a homecare agency like Vitalis HealthCare saves you from this as our caregivers are well vetted and further trained by our director of nursing before they are registered with us.
5. **Discuss Compensation Expectation & Offer:** Discussing their compensation expectations and presenting them with your offer is an important part of hiring a caregiver. This helps you determine which caregiver(s) fits your budget.
6. **Trust Your Instincts:** After reviewing and considering their qualifications, experience and other relevant factors, consider what your inner instincts may be telling you. If something doesn’t feel right, then maybe you should consider other caregivers and if something feels right, maybe you should consider hiring them for the role.
7. **Consider A Probation Period:** You can also discuss hiring your chosen caregiver for a probationary period of a few months. This can provide you with enough time to determine whether or not the caregiver you have hired is indeed the right fit for you and give you the option of hiring a different caregiver at the end of the probationary period.

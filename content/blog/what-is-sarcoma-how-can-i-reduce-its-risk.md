“Sarcoma” is a general term for cancers in the bones and soft tissues such as; muscles, fat, blood vessels etc. Approximately 16,000 sarcoma diagnoses occur in the United States yearly.
Sarcoma affects both adults and children. In general, soft tissue sarcoma often affects adults, and bone sarcoma often appears in children and teens.
Moving further, let's explore the risk factors associated with sarcoma.

* **Radiation:** exposure to high doses of radiation from other cancer treatments may increase the chance of developing sarcoma.
* **Exposure to chemicals:** exposure to certain chemicals used to make plastic, herbicides and even wood preservatives may increase the risk of sarcoma.
* **Genetics:** certain genetic makeup may increase the likelihood of getting sarcoma.

Now, what are some symptoms you should look out for? Signs and symptoms of Sarcoma vary depending on the location and size of the cancer. Additionally some sarcomas don't feel painful until they get big enough to press an organ.

1. Severe pain in the arm, legs and lower abdomen.
2. A new lump or growth that may or may not hurt.
3. Sudden weight loss
4. Severe and consistent back pain

Having one or all the symptoms above is not a proper diagnosis for sarcoma, if you or someone you know is worried about having sarcoma, reach out to a medical professional for proper test and diagnosis.
There are various treatment options for sarcoma, treatment depends on various factors including the size and location of the cancer.

1. **Surgery:** Through surgery, cancer cells are removed sparing as much healthy tissue as possible.
2. **Radiation therapy:** Radiation therapy uses high energy X-rays to kill cancer, advanced techniques are used to direct the radiation towards the cancer and spare healthy areas.
3. **Chemotherapy:** This treatment uses drugs that kill or reduce the growth of rapidly multiplying cancer cells.
4. **Immunotherapy:** This treatment strengthens the immune system so it is able to better identify and fight cancer cells in the body.

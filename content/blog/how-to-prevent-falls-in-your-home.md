Preventing falls at home is crucial for the safety and well-being of older people. Falls can lead to serious injuries, such as fractures or head injuries, and may impact a person's independence. Here are some practical tips to help prevent falls at home:

1. **Home Safety Assessment**: Conduct a thorough assessment of the home to identify potential hazards. Look for loose rugs, clutter, uneven flooring, and other obstacles.
2. **Remove Tripping Hazards:** Clear pathways of any tripping hazards, such as loose rugs, clutter, or electrical cords. Secure carpets and use non-slip mats.
3. **Improve Lighting:** Ensure that all areas of the home are well-lit, especially stairways, hallways, and entryways. Use bright bulbs and consider installing motion-sensor lights.
4. **Install Handrails and Grab Bars:** Install handrails on both sides of staircases and grab bars in the bathroom, especially near the toilet and in the shower or bath.
5. **Secure Loose or Uneven Flooring:** Repair or replace loose or uneven flooring, such as tiles or floorboards, to create a level surface.
6. **Use Non-Slip Flooring:** Choose non-slip flooring materials, particularly in bathrooms and kitchens. Non-slip mats or rugs can be added in areas prone to water or spills.
7. **Wear Proper Footwear:** Encourage the use of supportive, well-fitting shoes with non-slip soles. Avoid walking in socks or smooth-soled slippers.
8. **Organize the Home:** Keep commonly used items within easy reach to avoid the need for stretching or climbing. Consider rearranging furniture to create clear pathways.
